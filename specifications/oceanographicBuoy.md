# Oceanographic buoy specification

An oceanographic buoy is a meteorological monitoring system designed to collect and transmit data on sea state, wind conditions, and atmospheric parameters for weather monitoring and forecasting purposes. The buoy consists of sensors that measure environmental parameters, a controller that aggregates sensor data and computes derived parameters, and communication systems that enable transmission of both measured and computed data. The system to be designed is the software implementing the control logic of the buoy controller. The buoy shall transmit the following measurements via a VHF transmitter: buoy position, barometric pressure, wind direction, wind speed, wind gust, air temperature, seawater temperature, water level, relative humidity, direction of wave propagation, and wave energy spectrum. It shall be equipped with the following sensors: an IMU, a GNSS receiver, a barometer, an anemometer, an air temperature sensor, a water temperature sensor, and a humidity sensor. Wind gust shall be derived from anemometer data. Wave propagation direction and wave energy spectrum shall be derived from IMU data, and water level shall be derived from IMU and GNSS data. The model shall capture the control logic governing data acquisition, processing, and transmission, but shall not include the arithmetic computation of those derived values.
